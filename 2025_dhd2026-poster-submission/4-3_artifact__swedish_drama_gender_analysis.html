Gender Distribution Over Time

Key Findings:

Improvement in Female Representation: Female character representation increased from 39.5% in the 1880s to 44.7% in the 1890s, suggesting a gradual shift toward more balanced gender representation.

Author Influence: Female authors like Anne Charlotte Leffler, Alfhild Agrell, and Victoria Benedictsson typically featured higher percentages of female characters, with some plays having 70-80% female characters.

Genre Variations: Comedies and domestic dramas tended to have more balanced or female-dominated casts, while historical and political dramas (often by male authors) showed stronger male character dominance.

Gender Distribution by Decade

Notable Female Authors' Contributions:

Anne Charlotte Leffler: Consistently featured female-majority casts, with plays like "Elfvan" (71.4% female) and "Moster Malvina" (88.9% female).

Alfhild Agrell: Several plays with predominantly female characters, including "En hufvudsak" and "Hvarför?" (both 75% female).

Victoria Benedictsson: Contributed to more balanced gender representation with works like "I telefon" (62.5% female).

Methodology:

This analysis uses data from the DraCor (Drama Corpora) project, which provides structured metadata about dramatic texts. Gender distribution is calculated based on speaking characters only, excluding non-speaking roles. The percentages represent the proportion of female vs. male speaking characters in each play.

Data Source: Swedish Drama Corpus (SweDraCor) from the DraCor project

Limitation: Unknown gender characters (9.3% overall) are excluded from percentage calculations to focus on clearly identified gender distributions.
