Analysis Summary

Overall Pattern: The percentage of female speakers in Italian drama shows considerable variation over time, with no clear linear trend. The corpus spans nearly 500 years (1449-1933) and reveals interesting patterns in gender representation.

Early Period (15th-16th centuries): Shows relatively high female representation, particularly in religious and mythological works. Anonymous works and early Renaissance plays often featured substantial female roles.

Classical Period (18th century): Maintains good female representation, particularly in Goldoni's comedies and Metastasio's opera librettos, reflecting the importance of female singers in opera.

Neoclassical Period (late 18th-early 19th centuries): Alfieri's tragedies show balanced representation, while Romantic period works (Manzoni, Foscolo) show more variation.

Modern Period (20th century): Limited data suggests moderate female representation, though this requires more comprehensive analysis.
